## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

WTK Holdings Berhad is a Malaysian company primarily involved in timber and increasingly in oil palm plantations. Recent financial results show mixed performance, with revenue fluctuations and a shift from losses to profits in different quarters of 2025. The company is undertaking a significant RM555 million expansion into oil palm through acquisitions. Given these factors, a Business Expansion Loan and a Term Loan are potentially suitable to support their expansion plans. However, eligibility hinges on internal credit assessments and further due diligence.

## COMPANY ANALYSIS (from web sources)

- **Recent developments and activities:** WTK Holdings is actively expanding its oil palm plantation business through the acquisition of three companies for RM555 million [Web Source 3, 12, 17, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 25, 27]. These acquisitions are expected to be completed by Q1 2026 [Web Source 22, 24]. They also appointed new executives [Web Source 14].
- **Financial health indicators:** The company's financial performance in 2025 has been mixed. Q1 2025 showed a revenue of MYR 156.55 million but a loss before tax of MYR 17.531 million [Web Source 1]. In contrast, Q2 2025 showed positive EPS [Web Source 11, 13]. Full year 2024 revenue was MYR 671.91 million, a 19.81% increase from the previous year [Web Source 9]. Total liabilities increased slightly in Q1 2025 [Web Source 2].
- **Growth trajectory and expansion plans:** WTK is clearly focused on expanding its oil palm business [Web Source 3, 12, 17, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 25, 27]. This includes acquiring plantation land and a palm oil mill [Web Source 22]. The expansion will increase their planted area significantly [Web Source 23]. They also planned a RM1 billion Sukuk program [Web Source 15].
- **Specific financing needs identified:** The RM555 million acquisition plan [Web Source 3, 12, 17, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 25, 27] indicates a need for substantial financing. The acquisitions are expected to boost revenue and earnings [Web Source 22].

    *   **Type:** Secured Loan
    *   **Why this product is suitable:** Can be used to finance the acquisition of the three companies.

## LOAN OPPORTUNITY ASSESSMENT

-   **Estimated loan size and urgency:** RM555 million is needed for the acquisitions [Web Source 3, 12, 17, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 25, 27]. The acquisitions are expected to be completed by Q1 2026 [Web Source 22, 24], suggesting a relatively urgent need for financing.
-   **Specific use cases for each product:**
    *   **Business Expansion Loan:** Funding the operational costs associated with the expanded plantation area, including planting, maintenance, and harvesting.
    *   **Term Loan:** Financing the acquisition of Desacorp Sdn Bhd, Imbok Enterprise Sdn Bhd, and WTK Oil Mill Sdn Bhd [Web Source 22].
-   **Expected benefits to the customer:** Increased revenue and earnings from expanded oil palm operations [Web Source 22], vertical integration with the acquisition of the palm oil mill [Web Source 22].

## RM PROPOSAL STRATEGY

-   **Recommended approach for likely eligible products:** Focus on the Business Expansion Loan and Term Loan, given the company's clear expansion plans.
-   **Information to gather for verification:**
    *   Detailed financial statements (balance sheets, income statements, cash flow statements) for the past 3-5 years.
    *   Credit score and credit history.
    *   Details of the acquisition agreements for Desacorp, Imbok, and WTK Oil Mill.
    *   Business plan outlining the projected revenue and expenses for the expanded oil palm operations.
    *   AML/KYC documentation.
-   **Alternative products if primary choices may not be suitable:** If a direct loan is not feasible, explore options like a syndicated loan or a project finance facility specifically tied to the oil palm acquisitions.
-   **Key decision-makers to target:** Chief Financial Officer (Lilian Wong Zhi Hui) [Web Source 14], Executive Director (Lai Soon Ong) [Web Source 14], and other key members of the management team involved in the acquisition and expansion strategy.

## RISK FACTORS

-   **Potential concerns from public information:** Fluctuating financial performance in 2025 [Web Source 1, 11, 13] and increasing liabilities [Web Source 2] could raise concerns about the company's ability to service a large loan.
-   **Market or sector risks:** The palm oil industry is subject to price volatility and environmental concerns, which could impact WTK's profitability.
-   **Eligibility risks that need internal verification:** Creditworthiness, AML/KYC compliance, and adherence to internal credit policies.
