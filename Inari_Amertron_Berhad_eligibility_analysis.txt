## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Inari Amertron Berhad is a leading Malaysian technology company providing outsourced semiconductor assembly and testing (OSAT) services. While recent financial results show a decline in revenue and net income, the company is investing in advanced capabilities and pursuing strategic acquisitions like Lumileds to diversify its revenue streams and capitalize on growth opportunities in the semiconductor industry. Based on publicly available information, Inari Amertron appears to be a strong candidate for Equipment Financing and a Working Capital Loan. A Business Expansion Loan is also potentially suitable, but requires further verification of specific expansion plans and financial projections.

## COMPANY ANALYSIS (from web sources)

*   **Recent Developments and Activities:** Inari Amertron is actively involved in several key activities:
    *   **Acquisition of Lumileds:** Jointly acquiring Lumileds with San'an Optoelectronics to expand its optoelectronics segment [Web Source 4, 12, 17, 19, 27, 28].
    *   **Investment in Advanced Capabilities:** Increasing capital expenditure to approximately RM250 million in 2025-2026 to fund new technologies and expand capacity [Web Source 5].
    *   **Participation in Malaysia's National Semiconductor Strategy:** Aligned with the national strategy to foster high-value activities in the semiconductor industry [Web Source 21].
    *   **Expansion into EV-related semiconductors:** Pivoting towards EV-related semiconductors with expected revenue of RM80-100 million [Web Source 17].
*   **Financial Health Indicators:**
    *   **Revenue:** RM1.35 billion for FY2025, down 8.6% from FY2024 [Web Source 1, 2, 9]. RM1.48 billion for FY2024 [Web Source 6].
    *   **Net Income:** RM218.7 million for FY2025, down 27% from FY2024 [Web Source 1, 2].
    *   **Profit Margin:** 16% in FY2025, down from 20% in FY2024 [Web Source 2].
    *   **Total Assets:** RM3.401 billion [Web Source 13].
    *   **Total Equity:** RM3.17 billion [Web Source 6].
    *   **Cash and Bank Balances:** RM300.1 million [Web Source 6].
    *   **Debt:** Low debt levels [Web Source 13, 17].
    *   **Return on Equity (ROE):** 7.91% in 2025, a decrease from previous years [Web Source 20].
*   **Growth Trajectory and Expansion Plans:**
    *   **Revenue Growth Forecast:** Expected to grow 9.5% per annum over the next 3 years [Web Source 2].
    *   **Expansion in Memory Business:** Ramping up memory business with additional production lines [Web Source 6].
    *   **Expansion in China:** Ramp-up of its 54%-owned Yiwu Semiconductor in China [Web Source 6].
*   **Specific Financing Needs Identified:**
    *   **Capital Expenditure:** Increased investment in advanced capabilities requires funding [Web Source 5].
    *   **Acquisition Financing:** The Lumileds acquisition may require financing [Web Source 4, 12, 17, 19, 27, 28].
    *   **Working Capital:** To support ongoing operations and expansion [Implied].

## RECOMMENDED LOAN PRODUCTS

1.  **Equipment Financing:**
    *   **Type:** Term Loan or Lease
    *   **Suitability:** Inari is investing heavily in new equipment and advanced capabilities [Web Source 5]. Equipment financing directly addresses this need by providing funds specifically for acquiring new machinery and technology.
    *   **Key Features:** Tailored repayment schedules to match the equipment's revenue generation, potential tax benefits (depending on structure), and collateralized by the equipment itself.
2.  **Working Capital Loan:**
    *   **Type:** Revolving Credit Facility or Term Loan
    *   **Suitability:** While Inari has a healthy cash balance, a working capital loan can provide a financial buffer to manage day-to-day operational expenses, especially considering the recent revenue decline and ongoing investments [Web Source 1, 2, 9].
    *   **Key Features:** Flexible access to funds, short-term financing to cover operational gaps, and improved cash flow management.
3.  **Business Expansion Loan:**
    *   **Type:** Term Loan
    *   **Suitability:** Inari is actively expanding its operations through acquisitions (Lumileds) and organic growth (memory business, China expansion) [Web Source 4, 6, 12, 17, 19, 27, 28]. A business expansion loan can provide the necessary capital to support these initiatives.
    *   **Key Features:** Longer repayment terms, larger loan amounts, and structured to support specific expansion projects.

## LOAN OPPORTUNITY ASSESSMENT

*   **Estimated Loan Size and Urgency:**
    *   **Equipment Financing:** Loan size depends on the specific equipment being purchased. Given the planned RM250 million capital expenditure [Web Source 5], the loan size could be significant. Urgency depends on the timeline for equipment acquisition.
    *   **Working Capital Loan:** Loan size should be sufficient to cover short-term operational needs. Urgency depends on their current cash flow situation and upcoming expenses.
    *   **Business Expansion Loan:** Loan size depends on the specific expansion project (e.g., Lumileds acquisition, China expansion). The Lumileds acquisition is valued at RM1 billion [Web Source 19], suggesting a potentially large loan requirement. Urgency depends on the acquisition timeline.
*   **Specific Use Cases for Each Product:**
    *   **Equipment Financing:** Purchase of advanced semiconductor manufacturing and testing equipment.
    *   **Working Capital Loan:** Funding day-to-day operations, covering supplier payments, and managing short-term cash flow gaps.
    *   **Business Expansion Loan:** Financing the Lumileds acquisition, expanding production facilities in China, and supporting the ramp-up of the memory business.
*   **Expected Benefits to the Customer:**
    *   **Equipment Financing:** Enhanced production capacity, improved efficiency, and access to advanced technologies.
    *   **Working Capital Loan:** Improved cash flow management, ability to meet short-term obligations, and financial flexibility.
    *   **Business Expansion Loan:** Diversified revenue streams, increased market share, and access to new technologies and markets.

    *   **RF Division Struggles:** The RF division is facing challenges, including foreign exchange losses [Web Source 17].
*   **Market or Sector Risks:**
    *   **Semiconductor Industry Cyclicality:** The semiconductor industry is subject to cyclical fluctuations, which could impact Inari's revenue and profitability.
