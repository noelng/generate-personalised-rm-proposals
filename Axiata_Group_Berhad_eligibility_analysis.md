## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Axiata Group Berhad is a leading telecommunications conglomerate in Southeast and South Asia. Recent reports indicate a mixed financial performance, with revenue declines in some areas but overall profit growth driven by strategic gains, cost management, and asset disposals. The company is focused on improving cash flow, enhancing shareholder returns, and executing its 5\5 strategy. Based on publicly available information, Axiata appears to be a strong candidate for a Working Capital Loan and potentially a Refinancing Facility, although internal verification of credit ratings and specific financial details is necessary.

## COMPANY ANALYSIS (from web sources)

- **Recent developments and activities:** Axiata has been actively involved in strategic initiatives, including the XLSMART merger in Indonesia [Web Source 4, 9, 17], focusing on digital transformation [Web Source 9, 28], and exploring monetization of infrastructure assets [Web Source 2, 24]. They have also appointed new board members [Web Source 2, 4].
- **Financial health indicators:** Axiata reported a profit of RM431 million for 1H25 [Web Source 1, 5] and RM947 million for FY24 [Web Source 5, 7, 10]. While Q1 2025 saw a revenue decrease [Web Source 6], profit before tax increased [Web Source 6]. They also generated strong cash flow of RM2.3 billion in FY24 [Web Source 9]. Yahoo Finance reports a profit margin of 5.45% and an operating margin of 10.34% [Web Source 8, 20].
- **Growth trajectory and expansion plans:** Axiata is focused on becoming a Converged Connectivity Leader [Web Source 1, 9]. They are driving profit growth in frontier markets, realizing synergies from joint ventures, and building value in digital companies [Web Source 2]. The XLSMART merger is expected to strengthen their position in Indonesia [Web Source 9, 10].
- **Specific financing needs identified:** Axiata is prioritizing improved cash flow and enhanced yield [Web Source 1]. They are also aiming to reduce their gearing ratio [Web Source 9]. These objectives suggest a potential need for working capital optimization and refinancing of existing debt.

## RECOMMENDED LOAN PRODUCTS

1.  **Working Capital Loan:**
    *   **Type:** Short-term loan to finance day-to-day operations.
    *   **Why this product is suitable:** Axiata's focus on improving cash flow and operational efficiency aligns with the purpose of a working capital loan. It can help them manage short-term obligations and optimize their working capital cycle.
    *   **Key features relevant to this customer:** Flexible repayment terms, competitive interest rates, and potential for a revolving credit facility.

2.  **Refinancing Facility:**
    *   **Type:** Loan to replace existing debt with more favorable terms.
    *   **Why this product is suitable:** Axiata's goal to reduce its gearing ratio suggests they may be looking to refinance existing debt to lower interest rates or extend repayment terms.
    *   **Key features relevant to this customer:** Lower interest rates, extended repayment periods, and potential for consolidating multiple debts into a single loan.

- **Specific use cases for each product:**

    -   **Refinancing Facility:** Reducing interest expenses, extending debt maturity, and improving overall financial flexibility.
- **Expected benefits to the customer:**
    -   **Working Capital Loan:** Improved liquidity, enhanced operational efficiency, and better management of working capital cycle.
    -   **Refinancing Facility:** Lower debt servicing costs, improved credit rating (potentially), and increased financial flexibility.

## RM PROPOSAL STRATEGY

- **Recommended approach for likely eligible products:** Prioritize the Working Capital Loan and Refinancing Facility discussions. Highlight the bank's expertise in serving large telecommunications companies and its ability to provide customized financing solutions.
- **Information to gather for verification:** Credit score, detailed financial statements (including balance sheets, income statements, and cash flow statements), existing debt structure, and specific use of funds.
- **Alternative products if primary choices may not be suitable:** If a Refinancing Facility is not immediately viable, explore options for a Term Loan to fund specific projects or acquisitions.
- **Key decision-makers to target:** Tan Sri Shahril Ridza Ridzuan (Chairman) [Web Source 1], key members of the finance team, and potentially Didi Syafruddin Yahya (Director) [Web Source 2, 4].

- **Market or sector risks:** The telecommunications industry is highly competitive and subject to rapid technological changes. Regulatory risks and currency fluctuations could also impact Axiata's performance.
- **Eligibility risks that need internal verification:** Credit rating, compliance with internal credit policies, and the availability of suitable collateral.
